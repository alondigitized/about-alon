Retail Intelligence Pulse

Saturday, April 04 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

Executive summary

The signal today is narrower but useful. Three developments stand out: lululemon’s quarter sharpened the full-price and North America problem, Macy’s put a real early number behind AI-assisted discovery, and Walmart kept pushing retailer identity beyond the commerce site and into connected TV.

Bottom line: the durable pattern is not generic AI adoption. It is retailers and brands trying to protect full-price demand, owned discovery, and measurable identity as traffic fragments across more surfaces.

What changed since yesterday or newly matters now

Premium retail still has a full-price problem: lululemon’s latest results show international growth can mask a weaker North America and a margin structure under stress.
AI discovery is starting to produce concrete retail metrics: Macy’s says users of Ask Macy’s generated materially higher revenue per visit in beta, with the product framed as curated discovery rather than classic search.
Retailer identity is moving offsite: Walmart and VIZIO are making account-linked connected TV a commerce and measurement surface, not just an ad inventory surface.

Source-backed facts

lululemon: premium demand is still strong internationally, but North America and margin quality are the issue

Fact: lululemon said fourth-quarter fiscal 2025 revenue increased 1% to $3.6 billion.
Fact: lululemon said Americas net revenue decreased 4%, while international net revenue increased 17%.
Fact: lululemon said gross margin decreased 550 basis points to 54.9% in the quarter.
Fact: lululemon said inventories ended the year up 18% to $1.7 billion.
Fact: Reuters reported management made returning to full-price sales growth in North America a top priority, using product newness, SKU reduction, and inventory rebalancing to get there.
Fact: lululemon guided fiscal 2026 revenue to $11.35 billion to $11.50 billion, or 2% to 4% growth.

Macy’s: AI works when it improves discovery, not when it impersonates search

Fact: Retail Dive reported Ask Macy’s was formally released on March 23 after an internal dark launch in December.
Fact: During beta, revenue per visit was 4.75 times higher among customers who used Ask Macy’s than those who did not, according to a Macy’s spokesperson cited by Retail Dive.
Fact: Macy’s Chief Customer and Digital Officer Max Magni said the tool is “not about search” and instead about “curated discovery.”
Fact: Ask Macy’s is now available across Macy’s digital platforms, including its app.

Walmart + VIZIO: identity, login, and measurement are becoming part of media infrastructure

Fact: Walmart said it is introducing new content-to-commerce integrations and CTV campaign results at the 2026 IAB NewFronts.
Fact: AdExchanger reported that smart TVs using VIZIO’s operating system will require Walmart accounts to sign up and activate TV features.
Fact: Brave-indexed coverage of Walmart’s announcement describes the initiative around unified account logins, retail-data linkage, and closed-loop measurement rather than audience reach alone.

Implications for a footwear digital leader

Do not confuse top-line resilience with digital health. lululemon’s print is a clean reminder that premium brands can still post respectable revenue while getting weaker on the metrics that actually matter for long-term value creation: North America, full-price sell-through, inventory quality, and margin.
AI deserves budget only when it upgrades merchandising and discovery. Macy’s early number is directionally important because the use case is narrow and commercially legible: helping shoppers discover better baskets. That is far more credible than broad claims about AI “engagement.”
Membership and login are becoming media assets. Walmart is showing that retailer identity can travel into connected TV and still tie back to commerce outcomes. For footwear brands, that raises the stakes on where customer identity sits when discovery starts offsite.

Actions

1. Rebuild the weekly scorecard around full-price quality.
Track North America demand, markdown penetration, inventory aging, and product newness separately from overall revenue growth. lululemon is a warning against letting international strength hide domestic softness.
2. Test AI in discovery with a hard commercial hurdle.
Follow Macy’s logic: use AI where it can improve guided discovery, recommendation quality, and basket building. Hold it to conversion, revenue-per-visit, and attach-rate metrics, not chatbot usage stats.
3. Audit where your customer identity survives fragmented discovery.
If a shopper starts in retail media, CTV, marketplaces, or AI surfaces, map how fast you can reconnect them to membership, preferences, pricing integrity, and measurable conversion.
