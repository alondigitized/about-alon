Retail Intelligence Pulse

Sunday, May 10 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

Three concrete moves stand out. Target is scaling creator commerce from campaign tactic into shoppable infrastructure. Amazon is extending its logistics stack beyond sellers into a third-party service business. In footwear, Brooks just posted a very strong start to 2026, which raises the bar for On's May 12 earnings and reinforces that performance running demand is still concentrating in brands with momentum, not automatically flowing back to the biggest incumbents.

Target is operationalizing creator commerce, not just testing it

Influencer-led discovery is being wired directly into assortment and checkout

Target said on May 6 that its invite-only Target Creators program now includes more than 1,200 creators, over 40,000 products, and direct shopping across Target Plus, Instagram, and creator storefronts. Target said creators earn commissions when shoppers buy through their links and that the program spans home, fashion, beauty, and everyday categories.

Target Creators program expansion

This is strategically more important than a normal influencer update. Target is building a distributed demand-capture layer that sits between social discovery and owned checkout. For brands, that means creator readiness, content quality, and product-feed discipline are becoming part of retail account management, not just brand marketing.

Treat creator commerce as a channel capability. Align marketplace content, PDP assets, attribution, and retail media so social discovery can convert cleanly when retailers like Target turn creators into persistent storefront infrastructure.

Amazon is selling the machine behind its own retail advantage

Logistics is becoming a product for brands and retailers beyond Amazon's marketplace

Amazon announced on May 4 that Amazon Supply Chain Services opens its freight, distribution, fulfillment, and parcel shipping capabilities to businesses of all types and sizes, not only Amazon sellers. Amazon said early users include Procter & Gamble, 3M, Lands' End, and American Eagle Outfitters, and said the service includes a unified inventory pool, multi-channel fulfillment, and AI forecasting models to optimize inventory placement.

Amazon Supply Chain Services launch

Amazon is widening its control from traffic and checkout into upstream operating infrastructure. If brands let Amazon become the easiest answer for freight, inventory positioning, and parcel delivery across channels, the platform gains leverage well before the shopper ever sees a product page.

Pressure-test where your supply-chain stack is actually differentiated. If a third party can match or beat your speed, accuracy, and multi-channel inventory visibility, then logistics is no longer just an operations topic, it is a strategic dependency risk.

Performance running still looks stronger than the broader footwear reset

Brooks just put up a strong quarter, making On's May 12 print the next important read

Brooks said on May 1 that it delivered 23% global growth in the first quarter, including 20% growth in North America, 30% currency-neutral growth in EMEA, and 136% growth in China. Brooks also said it kept the number-one market-share position in U.S. specialty performance running footwear and held roughly 20% share in national retail for the 11th consecutive quarter.

Brooks Q1 2026 update

On said it will release first quarter 2026 results on Tuesday, May 12, 2026 before U.S. markets open.

On Q1 2026 earnings date

The category read is getting clearer. Performance running still has real demand and share movement, but the winners are the brands with product credibility and cleaner execution. That matters for Nike, adidas, New Balance, ASICS, Hoka, and others because this part of the market is not waiting for broad category recovery.

Use the next On print as a competitive benchmark, not just a financial update. Compare growth quality by geography, channel mix, and innovation narrative to judge whether performance demand is broadening or concentrating further.

Evidence caveat: Target, Amazon, Brooks, and On links above are company-issued materials, so the factual claims about launches, scale, and timing are strong. The strategic implications are interpretation. Brooks reported strong growth, but it did not publish full financial statements in this release, so comparability to public-company earnings disclosures is limited. On's source here only confirms the reporting date, not business performance.
