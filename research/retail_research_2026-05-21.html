Retail Intelligence Pulse

Thursday, May 21 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

The strongest signal in market this morning is not generic consumer resilience. It is that retailers with real convenience infrastructure and disciplined assortment are widening the gap. Target just showed that loyalty, marketplace, retail media, and same-day delivery can move together in the P&L. Amazon is going a step further by trying to intermediate purchases it does not even fulfill. Meanwhile, On is showing that premium footwear brands with full-price control and founder-speed operating models can still grow through tariff noise.

Target just produced the kind of digital growth wholesale brands should care about

Membership, same-day fulfillment, marketplace, and retail media are compounding together

Target reported on May 20 that first quarter net sales grew 6.7 percent, comparable traffic rose 4.4 percent, and digital comparable sales grew 8.9 percent, led by more than 27 percent growth in same-day delivery powered by Target Circle 360.

Target: Q1 2026 earnings release

Target also said non-merchandise sales grew nearly 25 percent, reflecting strong growth in Roundel ad revenue, Target Circle 360 membership revenue, and the Target+ marketplace.

Target: Q1 2026 earnings highlights

Reuters reported that Target raised its fiscal-year net sales growth outlook to around 4 percent from its prior 2 percent target, while management still warned about a stretched consumer and tougher conditions ahead.

Reuters via MarketScreener: Target raises growth outlook

This matters because it is a live proof point that digital growth is no longer just an ecommerce site story. The winning stack is membership plus fast fulfillment plus retail media plus third-party assortment. If a wholesale partner gets those loops right, it captures more demand before the brand ever sees the customer.

Pressure-test which wholesale accounts are actually becoming demand engines versus just distribution. For the strong ones, negotiate for better media access, richer item data, and more deliberate launch planning. For the weak ones, stop treating volume alone as strategic.

Amazon is trying to own shopping even when the sale happens somewhere else

Discovery, comparison, and even checkout are being pulled into Amazon’s interface layer

Amazon said Alexa for Shopping is now available to U.S. customers across the Amazon Shopping app, website, and Echo Show devices, with features including AI overviews, dynamic comparisons, price history, price alerts, auto-buying at target prices, and shopping guides that compare options across Amazon and the web.

Amazon: Alexa for Shopping rollout

Amazon also said its Shop Direct program now includes more than 100 million products from more than 400,000 merchants, and that tens of millions of products are available through Buy for Me, which lets Amazon agentically complete purchases on a customer’s behalf from brand websites using encrypted payment and shipping details.

Amazon: Shop Direct merchant feeds expansion

Amazon: Buy for Me feature

This is strategically more aggressive than a better search assistant. Amazon is building a control point above the transaction layer. If customers discover, compare, and authorize purchases inside Amazon even when inventory sits on a brand site, Amazon gains the behavior data and interface power while the brand risks becoming a fulfillment endpoint with weaker direct relationship leverage.

Audit whether your brand would benefit from Amazon-mediated discovery versus what it would cost in data visibility, repeat-relationship ownership, and pricing control. This is not just a marketplace decision anymore. It is an interface strategy decision.

On is showing what premium footwear resilience actually looks like

Full-price discipline and founder-led execution are still producing leverage

On reported May 12 that first quarter net sales rose 14.5 percent year over year, or 26.4 percent on a constant-currency basis, to CHF 831.9 million, while gross margin reached 64.2 percent despite higher U.S. tariffs. The company reiterated at least 23 percent constant-currency net sales growth for 2026 and raised its profitability outlook.

On: Q1 2026 results

In April, On said co-founders David Allemann and Caspar Coppetti would become Co-CEOs effective May 1, 2026, in a structure designed to connect founder-led strategic intent more directly with execution as the brand scales globally.

On: founders become Co-CEOs

The On signal is not just that premium running remains healthy. It is that a brand can protect margin, stay full price, expand DTC and wholesale together, and still accelerate if product heat and operating discipline are real. That is the more dangerous competitor profile for legacy footwear players than a simple promotional rebound.

Use On as a benchmark for where premium performance footwear is heading: tighter product focus, stronger full-price conviction, and operating models that keep senior leadership close to product and market signals. If decision loops are slow, the margin gap can widen fast.

Evidence caveat: the Target, Amazon, and On items rely primarily on first-hand company materials, which are strong on disclosed facts but self-interested. The Reuters report adds an outside read on how investors interpreted Target's quarter. The strategic conclusions here, especially on interface control and channel power, are interpretation rather than management guidance.
