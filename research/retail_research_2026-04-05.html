Retail Intelligence Pulse

Sunday, April 05 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

Executive summary

The cleanest new strategic signal is not a flashy earnings print. It is adidas’ newly published 2025 annual-report detail on how a winning footwear brand is actually balancing channels: wholesale is still the largest engine, DTC remains critical, and e-commerce is being managed for full-price quality rather than volume at any cost.

In parallel, Walmart is pushing retailer identity beyond the app and website into connected-TV operating systems. That matters because discovery, measurement, and conversion are increasingly being stitched together offsite.

Bottom line: the category is moving away from simplistic DTC-first ideology and toward channel coordination plus identity control.

What changed since yesterday or newly matters now

adidas published a fuller strategic channel readout: wholesale remained 60% of 2025 net sales, DTC was 40%, and management explicitly said it spent the last two years redefining e-commerce around higher full-price sales and lower promotional activity.
Walmart’s connected-commerce stack is getting more concrete: Walmart and VIZIO said new VIZIO OS and onn TVs powered by VIZIO will progressively get a Walmart-account login flow that links streaming engagement with retail interaction.
No equally strong fresh overnight disclosure from the primary footwear set surfaced in this run: that makes the adidas annual-report detail more useful today than another generic trend take.

Source-backed facts

adidas: the channel mix is more balanced than the old DTC-first narrative suggested

Fact: adidas’ 2025 annual report says wholesale remained the largest channel at 60% of total net sales, while DTC represented 40%.
Fact: adidas said all channels increased double digits in 2025.
Fact: adidas said it has “redefined the role of e-commerce” over the past two years and focused on improving full-price sales and reducing promotional activity.
Fact: adidas reported record 2025 revenue of €24.811 billion, with gross margin of 51.6% and operating profit of €2.056 billion.
Fact: adidas said footwear revenue grew 12% on a currency-neutral basis in 2025 and that growth was broad-based across Running, Training, Basketball, Sportswear, and Originals.
Fact: adidas said its market teams are empowered to tailor assortments, activations, partnerships, and business models locally, with local creation centers in places including Portland, Los Angeles, Shanghai, and Tokyo.

Walmart + VIZIO: retailer identity is moving into the device layer

Fact: Walmart and VIZIO said they are building a “scaled content-to-commerce solution” that combines storytelling, retail behavior, and closed-loop measurement.
Fact: Walmart said it will progressively implement a unified account login experience for new VIZIO OS TVs and onn TVs powered by VIZIO, allowing customers to use their Walmart account to access smart-TV features.
Fact: Walmart said the login framework is meant to connect streaming engagement directly with retail interaction.
Fact: Walmart said the system ties CTV ad engagement to purchases through closed-loop sales attribution.

Implications for a footwear digital leader

The market is quietly correcting the DTC-overreach of the last cycle. adidas’ channel disclosure says the winning model is not abandoning DTC; it is using DTC more selectively while treating wholesale as a strategic growth surface instead of a compromised one.
Full-price quality matters more than digital mix optics. If e-commerce growth requires constant promotion, it is not strengthening the business. adidas is being unusually explicit that digital needs to protect margin and brand heat, not just volume.
Customer identity is becoming infrastructure, not just CRM. Walmart is showing what happens when login, media exposure, and commerce outcomes converge inside a single ecosystem. Brands that do not control or at least reconnect identity fast will lose leverage as discovery fragments.

Actions

1. Re-score channel performance by contribution quality, not channel ideology.
Put wholesale, owned retail, and e-commerce on the same scorecard using full-price sell-through, markdown dependency, inventory health, contribution margin, and brand-right distribution quality.
2. Treat e-commerce as a precision instrument.
Audit where online demand is being bought with promotion. If digital volume is masking margin leakage, reset the role of the channel before the market does it for you.
3. Map where identity breaks across offsite discovery.
Connected TV, retail media, marketplaces, and AI-assisted discovery are all expanding. Know exactly how quickly a shopper can be brought back into membership, preference data, and measurable conversion once discovery starts elsewhere.
