Retail Intelligence Pulse

Thursday, March 26 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

Executive summary

The strongest new development is structural, not promotional. OpenAI’s March 24 commerce update made its direction explicit: Instant Checkout underdelivered, product discovery is the current priority, and merchants can use their own checkout experiences or build native ChatGPT apps. That matters because it clarifies where power is settling in AI commerce.

Bottom line: AI may increasingly own discovery, comparison, and referral, but retailers and brands are still fighting to retain checkout, loyalty recognition, payments, and post-click economics. For footwear, that raises the value of clean product data, inventory accuracy, member recognition, and strong landing experiences across brand and wholesale surfaces.

What actually changed since yesterday

OpenAI made the commerce pivot explicit. The important change is not another article interpreting the trend; it is the clearer articulation of the operating model itself: discovery first, merchant-controlled checkout second, optional native app experiences for deeper integrations.
Walmart’s ChatGPT experience is now more concrete. The latest reporting says Walmart’s in-ChatGPT app supports account linking, loyalty use, and Walmart payments, making the handoff model operational rather than theoretical.
ACP retailer participation is broader than the earlier Walmart-centric narrative implied. OpenAI’s latest rollout now explicitly names Target, Nordstrom, Sephora, Lowe’s, Best Buy, The Home Depot, and Wayfair as discovery integrations.

Source-backed facts

OpenAI / ChatGPT commerce model

Fact: Digital Commerce 360, citing OpenAI’s March 24 announcement, reported that OpenAI said the initial version of Instant Checkout “did not offer the level of flexibility” it wanted, so merchants are being allowed to use their own checkout experiences while OpenAI focuses on product discovery.
Fact: The same report said merchants that want deeper integrations can still develop native ChatGPT apps, rather than relying on the original one-size-fits-all checkout model.
Fact: The report also said OpenAI highlighted discovery integrations with Target, Sephora, Nordstrom, Lowe’s, Best Buy, The Home Depot, and Wayfair.

Walmart inside ChatGPT

Fact: Digital Commerce 360 reported OpenAI highlighted Walmart’s new in-ChatGPT app experience, including Walmart customer account linking, loyalty program use, and Walmart payments, with browser access already live and mobile app access scheduled to follow.
Fact: CNBC reported earlier that Walmart had already been working through the limits of native ChatGPT checkout and that OpenAI was shifting away from that earlier Instant Checkout approach.

Amazon’s parallel model

Fact: Amazon said Shop Direct now includes more than 100 million products from more than 400,000 merchants, and merchants can now sync catalog, price, and inventory data through feed partners including Feedonomics, Salsify, and CEDCommerce.
Fact: Amazon said customers can either click Shop Direct to leave Amazon for the merchant site or use Buy for Me on eligible items so Amazon completes the purchase on the merchant’s behalf.

Store and loyalty context still matters

Fact: Target said its March 25–27 Deal Days event gives free Target Circle members access to event savings and gives paid Target Circle 360 members 24-hour early access to select deals.
Fact: CNBC reported Walmart plans digital shelf labels across all U.S. stores by end-2026, reinforcing that pricing and store execution are being treated as real-time operating infrastructure, not just store ops.

Implications for footwear digital

Discovery is becoming more open; economics are not. AI assistants can widen top-of-funnel product discovery, but brands and retailers still want to preserve member recognition, payments, margin logic, and post-click merchandising on their own surfaces.
Footwear is unusually exposed to bad data. If AI-originated traffic hits stale inventory, weak size availability, poor fit attributes, or broken landing-page continuity, the customer experience collapses fast. Shoes are less forgiving than commodity replenishment categories.
Loyalty is hardening into access control. Target’s use of Circle and Circle 360 for event access is another reminder that membership is not just CRM garnish. It is increasingly tied to who gets value, speed, and priority.
The winners will connect AI discovery to operating reality. Walmart’s pricing infrastructure and Amazon’s feed-driven discovery model point to the same truth: product data, price integrity, inventory freshness, and fulfillment confidence matter more than glossy AI demos.

Actions

1. Audit AI-discovery readiness for top footwear franchises.
Check titles, attributes, fit/size data, imagery, price parity, inventory freshness, and landing-page continuity across brand.com, wholesale partners, marketplaces, and syndication feeds.
2. Define the handoff strategy.
Decide what should happen when traffic originates in ChatGPT, Amazon, or another external AI surface: loyalty recognition, assortment ranking, recommendations, payment options, and fulfillment promises should not be left accidental.
3. Treat membership as commercial infrastructure.
If loyalty only means points or discounting, it is strategically weak. Use it for early access, fulfillment advantages, personalized landing, and channel-sensitive product exposure.
