Retail Intelligence Pulse — footwear digital leadership lens

Prepared for Alan on 2026-03-11. Scope: primary footwear brands plus adjacent retailers. Standard: recent, source-backed, high-signal developments only. Important note: relative to yesterday, there is limited net-new primary-footwear disclosure. Today’s signal is concentrated in retail operating model, AI-commerce control, and short-cycle cost volatility.

1. Executive summary

The strongest new fact today is Amazon’s legal win to temporarily block Perplexity’s shopping agent from operating on Amazon. That is strategically important because it shows major platforms are moving to control how AI agents transact, not just how they search. The second useful development is a clearer read-through on Target’s 2026 operating model: membership, retail media, marketplace, and same-day fulfillment are growing faster than core merchandise sales, and Target is putting more capital behind store-led omnichannel execution. The third is macro: oil fell more than 11% in one session after de-escalation hopes in the Middle East, easing some immediate freight and cost anxiety but not eliminating supply-chain risk. For footwear, the implication is straightforward: the near-term battleground is controlled discovery, fulfillment economics, and margin discipline—not generic AI theater.

2. What changed today

  • Amazon won a temporary court order blocking Perplexity’s AI shopping agent from using Amazon’s platform.
  • Target’s 2026 plan now looks more concrete as an operating model: faster growth in membership, retail media, marketplace, and same-day fulfillment than in core merchandise, with a larger capital and store investment push behind it.

  • No major net-new disclosures from Nike, Adidas, New Balance, Puma, Skechers, On, Hoka/Deckers, Crocs, ASICS, or Brooks surfaced in the current scan. That itself matters: there is no fresh evidence this morning that changes the competitive read from yesterday on those brands.

3. Source-backed facts by company or theme

Amazon / AI shopping agents / platform control

Fact: Reuters reported that a California federal judge granted Amazon a temporary order blocking Perplexity AI from using its “agentic” shopping tool on Amazon’s platform. The judge said Amazon could likely prove the tool unlawfully accessed Amazon user accounts without permission, and found Amazon had presented “strong evidence” that Perplexity’s agent accessed Amazon’s systems illegally.
Source: Reuters, “Amazon wins order blocking access for Perplexity's AI shopping 'agent'” (March 10, 2026).

Target / loyalty / marketplace / retail media / fulfillment economics

Fact: In Target’s fourth-quarter and full-year 2025 earnings release, the company said non-merchandise sales grew more than 25%, membership revenue more than doubled, Roundel delivered double-digit growth, marketplace grew more than 30%, and same-day delivery powered by Target Circle 360 grew more than 30%.
Fact: Target said 2026 net sales should grow about 2%, with more than one percentage point of growth coming from new stores and non-merchandise sales rather than core comparable merchandise alone.
Source: Target corporate, “Target Corporation Reports Fourth Quarter and Full-Year 2025 Earnings” (March 3, 2026).

Target / store-led omnichannel operating model

Fact: Target said it plans to open more than 30 new stores in 2026, remodel more than 130 stores, launch next-day delivery in more than 20 new metro areas, and support the program with approximately $5 billion of capital investment in 2026.
Fact: In its broader strategy presentation, Target said it will invest an incremental $2 billion in 2026, including more than $1 billion in added capex and $1 billion in additional operating investment, while accelerating technology and AI to improve digital discovery and personalization.
Source: Target corporate, “Target Ramps Up Store Investments to Fuel New Chapter of Growth” (March 4, 2026) and “Target Outlines Strategic Plan for a New Chapter of Growth in 2026 and Beyond” (March 3, 2026).

Macro / cost environment / retail supply-chain sensitivity

Fact: Reuters reported Brent crude fell 11% to $87.80 a barrel and WTI fell 11.9% to $83.45 on March 10, the biggest single-day percentage decline since March 2022, after hopes of a quicker end to the Iran conflict. Reuters also noted that even if the conflict ends, oil supply recovery may be slow.
Source: Reuters, “Oil dives, settles down 11% after Trump predicts Middle East de-escalation” (March 10, 2026).

4. Why it matters for footwear digital

  • AI commerce is moving from interface experimentation to access control. If the largest marketplace can successfully restrict third-party AI shopping agents, brands should assume the future of AI-mediated commerce will be gated by platform permissions, APIs, identity rules, and commercial terms—not open browser automation.
  • Target is showing where retail profit pools are shifting. Membership, retail media, marketplace, and fulfillment-linked services are growing faster than core merchandise. For footwear brands, that means discoverability and economics inside retail ecosystems may matter as much as the product page itself.
  • Store investment is not anti-digital; it is the operating substrate for digital. Target’s strategy reinforces that stores, same-day services, and local inventory are still central to digital conversion and loyalty economics.
  • Cost pressure is less panicked than 48 hours ago, but not resolved. The oil move helps sentiment and may ease near-term freight concerns, but Reuters’ warning on slower supply recovery means sourcing and promotion decisions still need margin discipline.

5. Strategic implications

  1. Do not build AI commerce assumptions on uncontrolled agent access. Footwear brands should expect marketplaces and major retailers to decide which agents can browse, price-check, add to cart, and transact.
  2. Retail partners are increasingly monetizing the ecosystem around the sale. As membership, retail media, and marketplace services scale, footwear brands may need sharper investment frameworks for retailer media, loyalty-linked offers, and assortment placement.
  3. The omnichannel winner is the one with better local execution, not just better UX. Faster delivery claims, cleaner inventory visibility, and stronger store labor/training are becoming competitive digital advantages.

6. Recommended actions

  1. Audit AI-commerce exposure by platform. Map where your products can and cannot be discovered or transacted by AI agents across Amazon, owned DTC, and priority wholesale partners. Treat this as a channel-control issue, not just an innovation question.
  2. Re-rank retail partner priorities using ecosystem economics. For Target, Walmart, Amazon, and top wholesale accounts, evaluate not just sell-through but also retail-media leverage, loyalty access, marketplace rules, and same-day fulfillment contribution.
  3. Keep near-term pricing and inventory plans flexible. Use the oil reversal as breathing room, not a reason to relax. Maintain contingency plans for freight, input costs, and promotion cadence if energy or regional disruption spikes again.
