Retail Intelligence Pulse

Wednesday, May 13 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

Two signals matter this morning. First, On is still proving that premium performance footwear can grow without leaning on discounting or wholesale sprawl. Second, Target is treating stores, supply chain, and technology as one capital stack, which is a reminder that omnichannel advantage is still being built with physical assets, not just front-end UX and AI language.

On keeps compounding with premium demand and direct discipline

Strong growth, bigger DTC base, and no visible trade-down story

On Holding said on May 13 that first-quarter 2026 net sales rose 40.0% year over year to CHF 726.6 million on a reported basis, or 33.6% on a constant-currency basis.

On first-quarter 2026 results

The company said DTC net sales grew 45.3% to CHF 276.9 million and wholesale net sales grew 36.6% to CHF 449.7 million, while gross profit margin expanded 120 basis points to 59.9%.

On first-quarter 2026 results

On raised its full-year 2026 outlook and said it now expects net sales growth of at least 28% on a constant-currency basis, with gross profit margin in the range of 60.5% to 61.0%.

On first-quarter 2026 results

This is one of the cleanest current footwear signals in the market. The important part is not just growth. It is that On is still scaling both DTC and wholesale while protecting premium economics. That suggests performance running demand is still consolidating around brands with strong product energy and tighter marketplace exposure, rather than broad category recovery lifting everyone equally.

Pressure-test your own channel mix against On's pattern. If growth is showing up only where pricing is softer or inventory is wider, that is not the same thing as brand health. Separate demand quality from simple volume recovery.

Target is still funding stores and supply chain as digital infrastructure

$4 billion a year says omnichannel economics still depend on the physical network

Target said on May 12 that it plans to invest $4 billion to $5 billion annually over the next several years across new stores, store remodels, supply chain, and technology, and that it expects to add about 20 new stores in 2026.

Target financial community meeting announcement

The company said more than 95% of total sales are fulfilled by its stores and that its target is to add more than 300 stores over 10 years.

Target financial community meeting announcement

Target also said it is building new supply chain facilities, including food supply chain assets, and expanding sortation-center capabilities to support faster and more efficient delivery.

Target financial community meeting announcement

For footwear and retail digital leaders, the point is strategic, not just operational. The companies with the best digital economics are still the ones that can route demand through dense physical networks. Store fleets, last-mile capabilities, and inventory placement remain core digital advantages, especially as AI-driven discovery increases pressure to convert fast once demand appears.

Treat store network strategy, fulfillment economics, and digital conversion as one system. If those agendas still sit in separate planning lanes, you are probably underestimating where your real commerce moat or friction sits.

Evidence caveat: both primary sources above are company-issued materials, so the reported figures and capital plans are strong as factual disclosures. The strategic read-across is interpretation. On's premium running strength is not a proxy for the whole footwear market, and Target's investment plan signals intent, not guaranteed execution quality.
