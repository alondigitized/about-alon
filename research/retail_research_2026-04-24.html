Retail Intelligence Pulse

Friday, April 24 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

The strongest fresh signal is structural. PUMA just created a dedicated global wholesale leadership role, which is a clean admission that wholesale is not a legacy channel to be managed locally, but a strategic growth engine that now needs central control. At the same time, Amazon and Sam’s Club kept moving the market in the same direction from the platform side: Amazon is making it easier for outside merchants to plug inventory, pricing, and checkout into its AI shopping layer, while Sam’s Club is extending retail media and fast fulfillment deeper into the real-world member journey. The pattern is hard to miss. Channel control is consolidating around whoever owns the customer interface, the data link, and the operating infrastructure.

PUMA just made wholesale a top-level operating priority

New global wholesale role starts May 1

PUMA said on April 22 that it appointed Bertrand Blanc as Vice President Global Wholesale in a newly created role reporting directly to Chief Commercial Officer Matthias Baeumer, with responsibility for sales strategy, global revenue growth, and elevating the brand at major wholesale accounts. PUMA explicitly called wholesale a crucial part of its commercial engine and said the new role is meant to create a global centre of excellence for the channel.

PUMA, April 22, 2026

That is more than an org tweak. For athletic and footwear brands, it suggests the old posture of treating wholesale as a regional execution layer is breaking down. If accounts are becoming more data-rich, marketplace-like, and strategically uneven, brands need tighter central control over sell-in, assortment, merchandising standards, and account economics.

For brand leaders, the useful question is whether wholesale leadership is still fragmented across markets and account teams. If it is, you are probably under-managing one of the few channels that can still scale reach faster than owned digital.

Amazon is lowering the integration barrier for agentic commerce outside its own inventory

Shop Direct now pulls external merchants in through standard product feeds

Amazon said merchants can now connect product catalogs to its AI-powered Shop Direct experience through feed providers including Feedonomics, Salsify, and CEDCommerce. Amazon said Shop Direct now includes more than 100 million products from over 400,000 merchants, and that eligible items can be purchased through the agentic Buy for Me flow, where Amazon completes the purchase on the merchant’s site using the customer’s saved address and payment details.

Amazon, April 2026

This matters because Amazon is shifting from being only a retailer and marketplace to becoming a demand-routing layer for commerce more broadly. If brands can reach shoppers on Amazon even when inventory sits elsewhere, then discovery, comparison, and checkout orchestration are getting decoupled from where inventory is actually owned.

Treat product feeds, price accuracy, inventory freshness, and merchant-of-record rules as strategic infrastructure. In an agentic commerce world, weak catalog hygiene will become a growth tax, not a back-office nuisance.

Evidence caveat: the scale figures and merchant response are Amazon-reported. The strategic direction is clear, but Amazon did not publish conversion rates, take rates, or category-level performance.

Sam’s Club is turning membership, media, and fulfillment into one connected operating system

New media surfaces and faster club-based delivery both expanded on April 23

Sam’s Club said on April 23 that its MAP retail media business is expanding with enhanced Omni Experiences, Fuel Screen Ads, and In-Club Audio Ads. The company said earlier activations delivered a 24% sales lift for featured items last year. In a separate April 23 update, Sam’s Club also said its Express delivery service has already fulfilled nearly 65,000 orders, with an average delivery time of 55 minutes and its 10 fastest deliveries completed in under 12 minutes.

Sam’s Club MAP, April 23, 2026 · Sam’s Club Express Delivery, April 23, 2026

Sam’s Club is pushing a hard-to-copy model where the membership ID ties together audience targeting, in-club media, and local fulfillment. That matters for footwear and apparel brands because wholesale partners are increasingly selling not just shelf space, but measurable access to members plus operational convenience.

If club and mass partners can now prove lift and deliver in under an hour from local nodes, brands should revisit how they allocate exclusives, launch timing, and co-op spend. The issue is no longer just reach. It is who can convert attention into a transaction fastest and prove it cleanly.

Evidence caveat: both the sales-lift and delivery-speed figures come from Sam’s Club disclosures. They are useful operating signals, but not independently audited comparisons versus peers.
