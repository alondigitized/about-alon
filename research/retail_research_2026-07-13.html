Retail Intelligence Pulse

Monday, July 13 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

There was no major new overnight footwear earnings release by early Monday, July 13, 2026. The sharper live signal is that summer demand has already been pulled into late June and early July through retailer-led deal events, loyalty gating and AI-assisted discovery. For footwear leaders, that means back-to-school planning is no longer a late-summer question; channel readiness, promo discipline and marketplace visibility are getting tested now.

The summer retail calendar has moved forward and become more value- and membership-led

Amazon moved Prime Day 2026 to June 23-26, and Reuters reported on July 6 that Amazon, Walmart, Target and Best Buy all rolled out back-to-school promotions earlier than usual, broadening what used to be a late-summer shopping window into a longer summer sales season.

Reuters via Investing.com: Amazon Prime Day to gauge US consumer strain as focus shifts to basics
Reuters via MarketScreener: US back-to-school bargain hunt starts early as higher bills pressure families

Walmart said its Walmart Deals event ran June 22-28 with thousands of offers across top brands, popular categories and early back-to-school essentials, while Walmart+ members received early access to select high-demand deals.

Walmart: Walmart Deals Returns June 22–28

Target said more than 50% of its back-to-school assortment is new this year, with Target Circle members who are teachers or college students eligible for 20% off one storewide purchase from July 5 through September 12 and more than 50% off an annual Target Circle 360 membership.

Target: Back-to-school and back-to-college launch

The important shift is not just heavier discounting. Major retailers are compressing demand into earlier, membership-mediated shopping moments, which changes when brands need clean inventory, compelling newness and promotional clarity in market.

Move any remaining back-to-school footwear and apparel readiness checks forward. Audit July assortment depth, promo rules, loyalty offers, marketplace feeds and inventory availability now rather than waiting for August traffic.

AI-assisted retail discovery is now producing real conversion, not just curiosity

Adobe said U.S. retailers generated $26.4 billion in online spend during the June 23-26 Prime Day event, up 9.3% year over year, with mobile driving 54.2% of online sales.

Adobe: Prime Day 2026 insights

Adobe also said traffic from AI-powered chat services and browsers during the event was up 89%, and those visitors converted 40% better than non-AI channels. In the same event a year earlier, AI traffic converted 23% worse.

Adobe: Prime Day 2026 insights

Target said AI-powered recommendations in Target.com and the Target app now help surface student and teacher wish-list items and forgotten essentials during its back-to-school push.

Target: Back-to-school and back-to-college launch

This is the practical crossover point. AI discovery is starting to influence high-intent shopping behavior, and retailers are already wiring it into merchandising and list-building. In footwear, the brands that win will be the ones whose product data, availability and pricing survive that machine-mediated handoff cleanly.

Treat AI-surface readiness as a commerce issue, not a PR issue. Pressure-test product titles, attributes, imagery, inventory accuracy, loyalty hooks and handoff paths for both owned and partner surfaces before the August demand peak.

Upcoming footwear readouts will be judged through channel mix and promo discipline

Nike reported on June 30 that fourth-quarter wholesale revenue rose 4% to $6.6 billion while NIKE Direct revenue fell 7% to $4.1 billion, including a 12% decline in NIKE Brand Digital.

Crocs announced on July 9 that it will report second-quarter 2026 results on July 30.

Nike's quarter already reset the discussion: investors and operators are looking harder at where profitable growth is actually coming from. The next footwear readouts are likely to get read through three lenses: wholesale resilience, owned-digital quality and whether promotions are buying volume or masking weakness.

Build a two-week watchlist before Crocs and Puma report: DTC traffic quality, markdown depth, wholesale sell-through, marketplace mix and inventory health. Those are the signals most likely to matter more than headline revenue alone.

Source quality is mixed but usable. Adobe, Walmart, Target, Nike and Crocs are primary sources. The early back-to-school demand shift is Reuters reporting carried by syndication partners, which is credible but still second-hand. Puma's calendar confirms timing, not business performance.
