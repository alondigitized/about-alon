Retail Intelligence Pulse

Tuesday, May 26 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

The cleanest signal this morning is not generic AI rhetoric. It is that premium footwear winners are still compounding with margin discipline, and the next competitive layer is increasingly logistics and operating model. Deckers and On both posted evidence that strong brands can keep growing without obvious promotional damage. At the same time, Target is reloading supply chain leadership for a turnaround, and Amazon is selling its internal logistics stack as external infrastructure. For a footwear operator, that means brand heat still matters, but execution speed, inventory positioning, and channel-ready fulfillment are becoming more strategic by the quarter.

Deckers is still getting both growth and mix from HOKA-led momentum

That matters because it suggests performance demand is still translating into profitable DTC and international growth

Deckers said on May 21 that fourth-quarter fiscal 2026 revenue rose 9.6 percent to $1.119 billion, with HOKA net sales up 14.5 percent to $671.2 million.

Deckers Brands fourth quarter and full year fiscal 2026 results

Deckers also said fourth-quarter DTC net sales increased 13.2 percent to $464.4 million, DTC comparable net sales increased 8.2 percent, and international net sales rose 25.5 percent.

Deckers Brands fourth quarter and full year fiscal 2026 results

Gross margin improved to 57.6 percent from 56.7 percent, and the company guided fiscal 2027 revenue growth in the high-single-digit percentages.

Deckers Brands fourth quarter and full year fiscal 2026 results

This is more than a hot brand story. It shows that performance-footwear demand is still supporting stronger owned-channel economics and international scaling, not just wholesale sell-in. That raises the bar for incumbents that are still trying to rebuild digital productivity or margin quality at the same time.

Pressure-test where your own growth is coming from. If premium performance demand is rising faster than your owned-channel conversion, international availability, or fulfillment speed, the brand may be leaving margin and loyalty value on the table.

On is proving premium full-price discipline can survive tariffs and still scale

That is strategically more important than raw topline growth alone

On said on May 13 that first-quarter 2026 net sales increased 14.5 percent to CHF 831.9 million, or 26.4 percent on a constant-currency basis.

On first quarter 2026 results

The company said DTC sales increased 16.4 percent to CHF 322.3 million, wholesale sales increased 13.3 percent to CHF 509.6 million, and Asia-Pacific revenue increased 44.4 percent reported.

On first quarter 2026 results

On said gross margin reached 64.2 percent, up from 59.9 percent, and raised its full-year gross margin outlook to at least 64.5 percent even while embedding a 20 percent incremental tariff rate on products imported to the U.S. from Vietnam.

On first quarter 2026 results

The important point is not that On is growing. It is that the company is still defending premium pricing and expanding profitability while scaling DTC, wholesale, apparel, and APAC together. That makes the competitive threat look more structural than fad-like.

Track challengers by margin quality and channel balance, not just by sales growth. When premium brands keep expanding gross margin through tariff pressure, they are usually building stronger room to invest in product, media, and member experience.

Target and Amazon are both turning supply chain into strategy, but from opposite directions

One is trying to fix reliability for a retail turnaround, the other is monetizing logistics as a platform

Target named Jeff England chief global supply chain and logistics officer on May 19. Target said the next phase is about accelerating reliability, speed, and efficiency with technology and operational excellence, and England said the company needs to modernize how it plans, moves, and delivers products.

Target: Meet Jeff England, Target’s new chief supply chain and logistics officer

Reuters reported the same day that Target’s CEO is using leadership changes to drive efficiency and restore sales growth, after outlining a roughly $6 billion plan to improve inventory, in-store experience, and delivery times.

Reuters on Target supply chain leadership move

Amazon said on May 4 that it launched Amazon Supply Chain Services, extending its freight, distribution, fulfillment, and parcel shipping network to businesses beyond Amazon sellers. Amazon said Lands’ End is using a unified inventory pool to fulfill orders across multiple sales channels, and American Eagle is using Amazon’s parcel network for direct website deliveries.

Amazon Supply Chain Services launch

Amazon also said businesses using the service can tap its AI forecasting models and inventory placement capabilities across their own websites, marketplaces, social channels, and physical stores.

Amazon Supply Chain Services launch

This is the real operating-model signal. Retailers are no longer treating supply chain as a backstage function. It is becoming a growth lever, a service product, and a customer experience differentiator. Brands that cannot orchestrate inventory across their own site, marketplaces, wholesale, and stores will increasingly cede speed and availability advantages to the platforms that can.

Revisit whether supply chain is still managed as an efficiency silo. The better question now is whether your network, systems, and partner model are good enough to support faster promise dates, cleaner cross-channel inventory visibility, and more selective marketplace participation.

The macro backdrop still favors digital operators

Online continues to outgrow total retail, which raises the cost of weak digital execution

The U.S. Census Bureau said first-quarter 2026 retail e-commerce sales were $326.7 billion, up 9.8 percent year over year, while total retail sales increased 3.9 percent. E-commerce accounted for 16.9 percent of total retail sales.

U.S. Census Bureau quarterly retail e-commerce sales report

This is not company-specific, but it sharpens the context. Digital is still structurally outgrowing the store base, so any weakness in search, conversion, fulfillment, or CRM keeps compounding against the category, not just against one competitor.

Evidence caveat: Deckers, On, Amazon, and Target company statements are strong for reported facts and strategic intent, but they are still self-reported. The Reuters item adds useful outside framing on Target’s turnaround context, but it is still second-hand reporting rather than a direct management filing. The Census data is the strongest neutral benchmark in this brief.
