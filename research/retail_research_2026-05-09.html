Retail Intelligence Pulse

Saturday, May 09 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

The strongest fresh signal is that footwear is not moving as one market. Crocs just raised its full-year outlook on DTC-led strength. PUMA is still in transition, using marketplace expansion and DTC investment while intentionally shrinking parts of wholesale distribution in North America. At the same time, Amazon is making external-merchant discovery and agentic purchase paths easier to plug into its shopping surfaces. Net result: channel quality, product data readiness, and discovery placement matter more than broad category narratives.

Crocs is showing what a healthier channel mix looks like

DTC is carrying the growth signal even when wholesale stays pressured

Crocs said on April 30 that first quarter 2026 revenue fell 1.7% to $921 million, but DTC revenue grew 12.1% while wholesale revenue fell 9.9%. Within the Crocs brand, DTC grew 12.9% to $322 million. The company raised its full-year 2026 revenue outlook to a range of down 1% to up 1%, from prior guidance of down 1% to up slightly, and raised adjusted EPS guidance to $13.20 to $13.75 from $12.88 to $13.35.

Crocs Q1 2026 results and outlook

The important read is not just that Crocs beat expectations. It is that a footwear company can still absorb wholesale softness if brand heat, product newness, and owned-channel execution are strong enough. For peers, this raises the bar on digital merchandising, retention, and direct demand capture rather than just hoping wholesale reaccelerates.

Benchmark your current DTC contribution against your wholesale exposure by market and by franchise. If your direct business is not strong enough to offset partner volatility, fix search, assortment presentation, and lifecycle marketing before the next demand wobble exposes the gap.

PUMA and Nike together show the cost of channel and product reset

Wholesale cleanup and market-share pressure are still real, even when DTC and innovation pockets improve

PUMA said on April 30 that Q1 2026 currency-adjusted sales fell 1.0%. Wholesale declined 2.8% while DTC grew 3.8% to €528.1 million, raising DTC mix to 28.3%. It said e-commerce sales still increased 0.6% despite reduced promotions, helped by additional e-commerce marketplaces across APAC. PUMA also said its expected 2026 sales decline is mainly attributable to lower sales in North America reflecting measures to streamline distribution, while planned 2026 capex of about €200 million is focused on digital infrastructure and DTC channels.

PUMA Q1 2026 results

Reuters reported on May 5 that Nike's share of the global sports footwear market fell to 22.9% in 2025 from 25.9% a year earlier, while Adidas rose to 12.2% from 11.7%, based on Euromonitor data obtained by Reuters.

Reuters on Nike market share pressure

This is the sharper footwear story right now: some brands are still paying for prior overdistribution and weak product cycles, while others are using DTC and focused innovation to regain control. Marketplace expansion can help efficiency and reach, but it is not a substitute for fixing brand desirability and channel discipline.

Treat distribution quality as a strategic variable, not just a sales target. Audit where you are still buying volume through promotion, weak partners, or undifferentiated placement, then decide which doors, marketplaces, and categories actually build long-term pricing power.

Amazon keeps moving external commerce into its own AI-led discovery layer

Marketplace boundaries are getting blurrier, but Amazon still wants to own discovery and checkout intent

Amazon said merchants can now connect product catalogs to Shop Direct through third-party feeds including Feedonomics, Salsify, and CEDCommerce. Amazon said Shop Direct now includes more than 100 million products from over 400,000 merchants, that those products can surface in standard search and Rufus, and that eligible items can be purchased through the AI-powered Buy for Me flow, where Amazon completes the purchase on the merchant's website on the customer's behalf.

Amazon Shop Direct feed expansion

This matters because Amazon is extending from marketplace host to discovery orchestrator for inventory it does not even own. For brands and retailers, high-quality catalog, price, and inventory feeds are becoming prerequisites not just for marketplaces, but for AI-mediated commerce where the assistant chooses what gets surfaced and how frictionless the purchase feels.

Prioritize product-feed readiness as a board-level commerce capability. If your catalog, pricing, and inventory data are not clean enough for off-platform syndication and agentic purchase flows, you risk disappearing from the next wave of search and recommendation moments.

Evidence caveat: Crocs and PUMA are using company-reported results, so the financial facts are strong but the strategic framing is interpretive. The Nike market-share datapoint comes from Reuters citing Euromonitor data, which is useful directional evidence but still second-hand reporting rather than Nike disclosure. Amazon's Shop Direct details are company-supplied and concrete on scale and mechanics, but Amazon did not disclose conversion rates, merchant economics, or category mix.
