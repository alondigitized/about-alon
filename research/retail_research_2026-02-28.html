Daily Retail Research — 2026-02-28

Executive Summary

Latest signals across Nike, Adidas, New Balance, Puma, Skechers, On Running, Hoka, Deckers, Crocs, ASICS, Brooks Running, Amazon, Walmart, Target, Lululemon, Nordstrom, Macy's show accelerating digital adoption (DTC focus, AI, e-commerce, and omnichannel strategies). Key themes: AI-enabled personalization and optimization, AI-assisted shopping experiences, digital supply chain and inventory management, and the direct-to-consumer model as a margin lever. The data suggests three high-impact opportunities for a digital leader at a Fortune 500 footwear company.

Key Insights by Topic

  • Digital channels matter: Nike, Adidas, New Balance, Skechers, Deckers, Crocs, Brooks, and others highlight rising digital sales shares and DTC growth, with AI aiding marketing, product discovery, and inventory optimization.
  • AI as productivity and experience driver: AI for personalization, search, virtual try-ons, chatbots, and generative AI in marketing and operations are recurring themes across multiple brands.
  • E-commerce and omnichannel maturity: Brands are expanding direct-to-consumer platforms and leveraging omnichannel fulfillment to improve conversion and unit economics.
  • 3rd-party vs internal AI capabilities: There is a mix of AI pilots and scale, with leadership embedding AI product management, data governance, and cross-functional ownership.
  • Platform and ecosystem investments: Cloud-based AI tools, AI-enabled search, inventory optimization, and digital twins/3D design accelerate product development and time-to-market.

3 Actionable Opportunities for a Digital Leader

  1. Build a unified AI-driven consumer experience platform
    • Goal: Personalize product discovery, recommendations, and content across web/mobile/retail using AI.
    • Strategic moves: Implement an AI-powered merchandising and search stack; integrate with CRM and loyalty to deliver consistent experiences; pilot virtual try-on and generative content generation for product pages.
    • KPIs: AI-assisted conversion rate, average order value, digital revenue share, repeat purchase rate.
  2. Scale DTC and digital supply chain optimization with AI
    • Goal: Improve margins and on-time delivery through demand forecasting, inventory optimization, and agile planning.
    • Strategic moves: Adopt a centralized Digital Brain for demand sensing, integrate 3D design and virtual prototyping for faster product iteration, and deploy AI-driven logistics cost reductions.
    • KPIs: DTC revenue growth, gross margin, inventory turns, stock-out reduction.
  3. Accelerate data governance and AI product management
    • Goal: Create accountable AI products with clear ownership, governance, and ROI tracking.
    • Strategic moves: Establish cross-functional AI product teams, formal data governance, and a ‘AI ROI playbook’ to measure outcomes tied to commercial metrics.
    • KPIs: AI project ROI, adoption rate by functions, data quality metrics, time-to-value for AI pilots.
