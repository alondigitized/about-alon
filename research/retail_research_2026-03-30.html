Retail Intelligence Pulse

Monday, March 30 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

Executive summary

The clearest new retail signal is not a footwear earnings print. It is the shape of AI commerce becoming more concrete.

Amazon is scaling an AI-assisted external-store shopping layer through Shop Direct and Buy for Me. OpenAI + Walmart are pushing discovery inside ChatGPT while preserving retailer-owned account linking, loyalty, and payments. Macy's says customers using its new Gemini-powered shopping assistant are spending materially more online, while Target is explicitly funding digital discovery, loyalty, AI personalization, and faster fulfillment.

Bottom line: discovery is fragmenting across AI surfaces, but the economic value is still accruing to retailers that keep control of merchandising data, identity, checkout, and fulfillment promises.

What actually changed or newly matters now

Amazon is making external-store distribution more scalable: Shop Direct now supports third-party product feeds, making it easier for brands and retailers to syndicate catalog, price, and inventory into Amazon-controlled discovery.
Retailer-owned AI assistants are starting to show harder commercial evidence: Macy's says users of its new Ask Macy's chatbot spend about 4.75x more than non-users.
Large retailers are converging on the same capability stack: AI-driven discovery, personalization, loyalty, and fulfillment are being funded together rather than as isolated experiments.

Source-backed facts

Amazon is broadening AI discovery beyond its own first-party assortment

Fact: Amazon said Shop Direct helps customers discover and buy products from stores across the web on Amazon.com and in the Amazon shopping app.
Fact: Amazon said merchants can now connect catalogs through third-party feed providers including Feedonomics, Salsify, and CEDCommerce, syncing catalog, pricing, and inventory in real time.
Fact: Amazon said Shop Direct now includes more than 100 million products from more than 400,000 merchants.
Fact: Amazon said customers can either click Shop Direct to go to the merchant website or, for eligible products, use Buy for Me so Amazon's AI completes the purchase on the merchant's site using the customer's Amazon payment and address information.
Fact: Amazon said Shop Direct inventory can surface through traditional search as well as Rufus, its AI shopping assistant.

OpenAI and Walmart are reinforcing the same structural pattern from another direction

Fact: OpenAI said it is expanding the Agentic Commerce Protocol to support product discovery, with merchants sharing product feeds and promotions so their catalogs are represented in ChatGPT.
Fact: OpenAI said the initial version of Instant Checkout did not provide enough flexibility, so merchants are being allowed to use their own checkout experiences while OpenAI focuses on discovery.
Fact: OpenAI said Walmart is introducing an in-ChatGPT app experience that supports account linking, loyalty, and Walmart payments.

Macy's is offering one of the first concrete retailer-side conversion signals

Fact: Bloomberg reported on March 26 that Macy's launched Ask Macy's, a shopping assistant powered by Google Gemini.
Fact: Bloomberg reported Macy's said customers using Ask Macy's spent about 4.75 times more than those who did not use the tool.
Fact: PYMNTS, citing Bloomberg, reported the tool launched across Macy's digital platforms on March 23 and has been used notably for outfit completion and virtual try-on related shopping flows.

Target is allocating capital where the winners are likely to compete

Fact: Target said its 2026 plan includes $2 billion of incremental investment, including more than $1 billion of additional capital expenditure and $1 billion of incremental operating investment.
Fact: Target said it is increasing spend on brand marketing and new technology, including AI, to improve discovery and personalization.
Fact: Target said it plans to scale Target Circle 360, Roundel, and Target Plus while enhancing same-day services, which account for two-thirds of digital sales, and expanding next-day brown-box delivery to 20 new metro areas.

Implications for footwear digital

The new battleground is machine-readable merchandising. If discovery is increasingly brokered by Amazon, ChatGPT, Rufus, or retailer copilots, brands with weak product attributes, inconsistent pricing, poor inventory signals, or thin promotional metadata will lose visibility before a shopper ever reaches a PDP.
Retailers are not surrendering the transaction layer. Amazon, OpenAI, and Walmart are all converging on versions of the same idea: AI can intermediate discovery, but checkout, loyalty, payment, fulfillment, and service still need retailer-grade control.
For footwear brands, marketplace strategy is now inseparable from AI strategy. The question is no longer just whether to sell on Amazon or via wholesale partners. It is whether your assortment and customer value proposition are legible inside AI-mediated discovery environments controlled by others.
Macy's result is interesting but not yet definitive. A 4.75x spend delta is directionally important, but without basket-size controls, user mix detail, and repeatability data, it should be treated as an encouraging early signal rather than settled proof.

Actions

1. Run an AI-discovery readiness audit on core footwear assortments.
Check whether hero products have complete attributes, fresh imagery, review density, current price and promo metadata, variant clarity, and real-time inventory signals suitable for external AI surfaces.
2. Defend identity and membership at the moment discovery leaves owned channels.
Walmart's architecture is the tell. If shoppers start in AI, the winners will reconnect them quickly to account, benefits, and payment without destroying conversion.
3. Treat onsite AI assistants as a conversion program, not a lab demo.
If Macy's early numbers hold directionally, retailer- and brand-owned assistants can become a meaningful lever for outfit building, cross-sell, and fit-oriented shopping journeys that matter disproportionately in footwear and apparel.
