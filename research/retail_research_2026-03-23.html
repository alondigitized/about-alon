Retail Intelligence Pulse

Date: 2026-03-23 | Lens: footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces, loyalty, merchandising, omnichannel, AI-enabled discovery

1. Executive summary

The highest-signal change is not a footwear earnings surprise today. It is the accelerating shift in where product discovery starts and who controls the customer handoff.

  • Walmart/OpenAI: CNBC reported OpenAI is moving away from native chatbot checkout toward retailer apps, while Walmart said Sparky is expected to integrate into ChatGPT as soon as next week.
  • Amazon: Amazon expanded merchant participation in Shop Direct via third-party feeds, scaling external-catalog discovery inside Amazon and Rufus.
  • Target: Target is pairing a large 2026 investment plan with visible app-level AI shopping upgrades, showing a more serious push to connect digital discovery, loyalty, and in-store use.
  • Lululemon: Recent Q4 reporting reinforced that digital remains a major profit and growth support lever even when North America physical productivity softens.

Bottom line: the battle is shifting from “who has ecommerce” to “who owns discovery, intent data, and the checkout redirect.” For footwear brands, that matters because conversion, assortment visibility, loyalty identity, and margin control now depend on how well product data, inventory, and membership value travel across AI-assisted channels.

4. Why it matters for footwear digital

Discovery is fragmenting. Consumers can now find product through retailer apps, marketplaces, AI assistants, and agentic layers. That raises the value of clean product feeds, real-time inventory, structured attributes, and pricing integrity.
Checkout control still matters. OpenAI's retreat from native checkout is a reminder that retailers and brands still want the customer relationship, loyalty recognition, merchandising context, and basket economics that come with finishing the transaction on owned surfaces.
Mobile is now an in-store operating system, not just a commerce channel. Target's app changes matter because they blend repeat purchase, navigation, and AI assistance inside the store trip. For footwear, this points toward fit help, style completion, store inventory lookup, appointment/clienteling, and membership recognition as the next practical battlegrounds.
Digital resilience matters when store productivity softens. Lululemon's Q4 shows digital can offset pressure elsewhere, but only if online experience, assortment visibility, and full-price conversion stay strong.

5. Strategic implications

  1. Agentic commerce is becoming a data-distribution problem before it becomes a UX problem. If your product, inventory, pricing, and loyalty signals are not machine-readable and portable, AI-assisted discovery will route around you.
  2. Footwear brands will need sharper channel logic. Not every SKU should travel equally across DTC, wholesale dot-com, marketplaces, Amazon external discovery, and AI shopping assistants. Premium and launch product need tighter control; replenishment and long-tail styles may benefit from wider discoverability.
  3. Loyalty value must survive outside owned search boxes. If discovery starts in Rufus, ChatGPT, or a retailer app, the winner is the brand/retailer that can still recognize the member, preserve pricing and availability trust, and personalize the redirect or landing experience.

6. Recommended actions

1. Audit product-feed readiness for AI-assisted discovery.
Check whether key footwear catalogs expose accurate attributes, size/fit metadata, inventory, price, colorways, and availability in ways that can travel cleanly into marketplace, retail-media, and AI-shopping surfaces.
2. Define a channel-control framework for agentic commerce.
Decide which product classes should be discoverable broadly vs. guarded tightly, and what the redirect/landing experience should do for members, launches, and premium assortments.
3. Reprioritize mobile as an omnichannel productivity layer.
Use Target's app moves as a benchmark: build or sharpen high-frequency utilities such as buy-it-again, in-store wayfinding, personalized restock, fit guidance, and store inventory confidence rather than chasing generic chatbot features.
