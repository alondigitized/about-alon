Retail Intelligence Pulse

Saturday, July 11 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

There was no major new overnight earnings filing or strategic press release from Nike, adidas, New Balance, Puma, Skechers, On, Hoka, Deckers, Crocs, ASICS or Brooks by early Saturday, July 11, 2026. The stronger live signal is operational: AI-assisted shopping is now converting materially better during major retail events, while large footwear brands are still investing in the fulfillment, B2B and commerce-platform infrastructure required to capture that demand without losing control of the customer experience.

AI traffic is no longer just top-of-funnel noise

Adobe said U.S. retailers generated $26.4 billion in online spend during the June 23–26 Prime Day event, up 9.3% year over year.

Adobe Prime Day 2026 analysis

Adobe also said shoppers who arrived from AI-powered chat services and browsers converted 40% better than non-AI channels during the event, versus converting 23% worse in the comparable event a year earlier.

Adobe Prime Day 2026 analysis

Apparel discounts peaked at 24% during the event, while mobile drove 54.2% of online sales.

Adobe Prime Day 2026 analysis

That is the important change. AI discovery is no longer just sending curiosity clicks; it is sending shoppers who are more ready to buy. For footwear brands, that raises the value of structured product data, clean inventory availability, strong PDP content and promotion logic that can survive being surfaced by third-party agents rather than only by brand-owned search and navigation.

Treat AI-agent readiness as a conversion program, not a PR theme. Audit feed quality, stock accuracy, attribute completeness, pricing consistency and checkout handoff across both owned and partner channels before holiday planning hardens.

Nike’s live org signals say omnichannel pickup and wholesale digitization are still active build priorities

Nike’s current careers site includes a Beaverton-based “Lead, E-Commerce - Global O2O” role that says Nike Direct Digital Commerce is building Buy Online, Pick Up In Store and Pick Up Point services across digital product, stores, operations, supply chain and technology.

Nike Lead, E-Commerce - Global O2O job posting

The same posting says the role owns strategy and execution for new pickup formats including in-store counters, dedicated pickup zones, lockers and curbside.

Nike Lead, E-Commerce - Global O2O job posting

Nike also has a current Beaverton-based “Lead, Global Sales, Nike.net, New Business” role whose stated focus is to grow Nike’s B2B e-commerce business and accelerate wholesale transformation.

Nike Lead, Global Sales, Nike.net, New Business job posting

Nike’s own live hiring suggests it is still treating omnichannel fulfillment and digital wholesale enablement as unfinished strategic work, not solved capabilities. That matters because it reinforces where competitive advantage is moving: less in homepage theater, more in pickup reliability, partner onboarding, and the ability to digitize wholesale without recreating old friction.

Benchmark your pickup proposition and wholesale digital tooling against where Nike appears to be investing now. If store pickup, locker flows, partner onboarding or distributor self-service still feel secondary internally, that is probably a strategic lag, not a sequencing choice.

Puma’s current hiring points to a deeper rebuild of the commerce stack

Puma’s current posting for “Director of Engineering - Core Commerce Platform” describes a platform-first, API-first DTC architecture spanning checkout, payments, cross-channel inventory, omnichannel order management, post-order experience, membership and loyalty, and social and agentic commerce.

Puma Director of Engineering - Core Commerce Platform job posting

The posting explicitly names Salesforce Commerce Cloud, Fluent Commerce, APIGEE, payment integrations, real-time loyalty tiers and rewards redemption, and AI use cases such as intelligent checkout assistance, automated order exception handling and demand-aware inventory allocation.

Puma Director of Engineering - Core Commerce Platform job posting

It also frames social and agentic commerce as part of the core platform, including conversational commerce, social checkout integrations and AI agents for purchase assistance and order management.

Puma Director of Engineering - Core Commerce Platform job posting

Even allowing for hiring-language inflation, Puma is signaling a serious architectural direction: commerce, loyalty, OMS and AI are being designed as one connected system. For footwear operators, that is strategically more important than another brand campaign because it suggests the next battleground is stack coherence across discovery, checkout, fulfillment and member value.

Pressure-test whether your commerce stack is genuinely composable or just fragmented under nicer language. If loyalty, OMS, search, payments and conversational discovery still live in separate governance lanes, you are likely slower than the market direction implied here.

Source quality is mixed by design today. The Adobe data is strong, recent and directly measured. The Nike and Puma sections rely on current job postings, which are attributable and strategically useful but are weaker than an earnings call or formal launch announcement because they show intended investment areas, not completed deployment.
