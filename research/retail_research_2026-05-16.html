Retail Intelligence Pulse

Saturday, May 16 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

Two developments stand out. First, Amazon has made Shop Direct materially easier for brands to join by accepting the same product feeds many already use elsewhere, which pushes external brand inventory directly into Amazon search and AI shopping surfaces. Second, On's latest quarter shows a premium footwear brand can still expand margins while growing both DTC and wholesale, which is strategically important because it suggests the next battleground is not channel mix ideology, but whether brands can maintain pricing power and operational precision across channels.

Amazon is lowering the integration cost of letting its AI and search surfaces intermediate branded demand

Shop Direct is becoming easier to plug into, and therefore harder to ignore

Amazon said Shop Direct now includes more than 100 million products from over 400,000 merchants, and that merchants can now use existing feeds from partners including Feedonomics, Salsify, and CEDCommerce to sync catalog, pricing, and inventory in real time with Shop Direct.

Amazon: Shop Direct external stores update

Amazon also said those feeds can place products into traditional search results as well as Rufus, and that Shop Direct is available for all U.S. customers on Amazon.com, the Amazon Shopping app, mobile web browsers, and in Rufus.

Amazon: distribution across search and Rufus

For eligible items, Amazon's Buy for Me flow can complete the purchase on the customer's behalf using encrypted payment and shipping details, while the merchant still handles delivery, returns, exchanges, and customer service.

Amazon: Buy for Me operating model

This is not just incremental traffic syndication. Amazon is trying to become the intent-capture and decision layer even when the inventory sits outside its first-party assortment. For footwear brands, that means the digital shelf is shifting from owned PDPs toward assistant-mediated comparison, where structured product data, real-time inventory, and price integrity matter more than branded journey design alone.

Audit which product attributes, inventory signals, and pricing rules would actually travel well into assistant-led discovery. If a shopper can discover your product through Amazon before reaching your own experience, your metadata quality is now a channel strategy issue, not just an e-commerce hygiene issue.

On is showing that premium footwear can keep scaling without choosing between DTC and wholesale

Margin expansion with growth across both channels is the real signal

On reported first-quarter net sales of CHF 831.9 million, up 26.4% on a constant-currency basis, with DTC net sales up 28.7% on a constant-currency basis to CHF 322.3 million and wholesale net sales up 25.1% on the same basis to CHF 509.6 million.

On: Q1 2026 results

On said gross profit margin reached 64.2%, up from 59.9%, and raised its full-year gross profit margin outlook to at least 64.5% and adjusted EBITDA margin outlook to 19.5% to 20.0%.

On: raised profitability outlook

Reuters reported that Asia-Pacific was On's fastest-growing region at 61.4% constant-currency growth, while leadership shifted on May 1 to co-founders David Allemann and Caspar Coppetti as joint CEOs, with former Ahold Delhaize executive Frank Sluis joining as CFO.

Reuters via Investing.com: On raises margin goal

The useful lesson is not that DTC beats wholesale or vice versa. It is that a premium footwear brand with strong product heat, pricing discipline, and operational control can keep both engines working at once. That should make incumbents more skeptical of simplistic channel-mix narratives and more focused on whether each channel is reinforcing brand equity, not just chasing volume.

Use On as a benchmark for a tougher internal question: where is your margin structure improving because of true premium demand and operational execution, versus being temporarily protected by promotions, product mix, or partner terms? If you cannot answer that cleanly, you are probably overestimating channel health.

Evidence caveat: Amazon and On metrics above come from company disclosures, so they are first-hand but self-reported. The On leadership and regional context also draws on Reuters reporting, which is credible but still second-hand. The competitive read-across to broader footwear strategy is analysis, not disclosed fact.
