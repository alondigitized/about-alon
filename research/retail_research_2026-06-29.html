Retail Intelligence Pulse

Monday, June 29 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

As of Monday, June 29, 2026 at 4:00 AM PT, the strongest fresh signal is that retail competition is moving beyond the owned e-commerce site. Walmart is extending commerce media into connected TV with closed-loop measurement and a clearer path for marketplace sellers. Target is turning back-to-school into an earlier loyalty and membership capture moment, supported by real digital momentum in same-day delivery. In footwear, ASICS is showing that faster product cycles now depend on operating model design as much as product design: regional R&D nodes, AI-assisted development workflows, and brand-level structural autonomy.

Walmart is building a full-funnel media engine, not just a retail media network

Walmart said on June 23 that it plans to acquire Vibe.co, a self-serve connected TV advertising platform, to make TV advertising more accessible and measurable for small and mid-sized businesses and mid-market brands.

Walmart, June 23, 2026

Walmart said Vibe.co will be combined with Walmart Connect's commerce audiences, closed-loop measurement and VIZIO-backed media ecosystem, with the expected benefit extending specifically to Walmart's third-party marketplace sellers.

Walmart, June 23, 2026

One day earlier, Walmart said global advertising revenue grew 37% in its most recent quarter, while Walmart Connect U.S. grew 44% excluding VIZIO, and framed commerce media as a growth driver alongside ecommerce, membership and marketplace.

Walmart, June 22, 2026

This is strategically bigger than an ad-tech tuck-in. Walmart is turning its media stack into a customer-acquisition and demand-shaping service that can reach beyond walmart.com, while tying spend back to commerce outcomes. That raises the competitive bar for brands that still treat retail media as mainly search bids and sponsored listings.

For footwear and apparel leaders, the question is no longer whether retail media matters. It is whether marketplace, media, and measurement teams are operating as one growth system before Walmart and Amazon make that integration table stakes.

Target is trying to own the start of back-to-school through loyalty, convenience and AI-assisted planning

Target said on June 24 that more than 50% of its back-to-school and back-to-college assortment is new this year, with thousands of exclusive items across apparel, school supplies and dorm categories.

Target, June 24, 2026

Target also said teachers and college students in its free Target Circle program can get 20% off one storewide purchase from July 5 through September 12, while verified teachers and students can buy an annual Target Circle 360 membership for $49 versus the regular $99 price.

Target, June 24, 2026

Target said in its May 21 first-quarter earnings release that digital comparable sales grew 8.9%, led by more than 27% growth in same-day delivery powered by Target Circle 360, while non-merchandise revenue grew nearly 25% on strong growth in Roundel, Target Circle 360 membership revenue and the Target+ marketplace.

Target Q1 earnings, May 21, 2026

Target also said its app and website now use AI-powered recommendations for student and teacher wish lists.

Target, June 24, 2026

Target is not entering back-to-school as a simple promo retailer. It is bundling trend-right assortment, membership conversion, same-day fulfillment and AI-assisted basket building into one seasonal acquisition play. That matters because the season is increasingly won before the consumer reaches a generic search bar.

Pressure-test whether your own seasonal playbooks treat loyalty, fulfillment and discovery as connected levers. If merchandising, CRM and digital product are still sequencing these moves instead of stacking them, Target is operating faster.

ASICS is treating operating structure and AI workflow as competitive advantages in footwear

ASICS said on June 16 that it established the ASICS Institute of Sport Science Europe within ASICS Europe and partnered with HumanFab, giving it access to an advanced indoor tennis-court lab and a dedicated regional R&D presence in one of its key growth markets.

ASICS, June 16, 2026

ASICS said the setup will allow richer athlete data capture, more responsive product development, and included an early prototype of a next-generation tennis shoe shown at its Global Tennis Summit.

ASICS, June 16, 2026

On June 18, ASICS Ventures said it is working with RebuilderAI on an AI-powered workflow that moves from design concept to 3D CAD data and simulation, with the stated goal of reducing time and effort between idea, validation and market-ready design.

ASICS Ventures, June 18, 2026

Separately, ASICS said on June 10 that it will reorganize Onitsuka Tiger under OT GROUP with effect scheduled for January 1, 2027, aiming for faster decision-making, clearer accountability and continued expansion of a brand built around directly operated stores. ASICS said Onitsuka Tiger currently operates in about 160 countries and regions with approximately 190 directly operated stores.

ASICS, June 10, 2026

The deeper point is that footwear innovation is no longer just about hero-product launches. ASICS is compressing the loop between athlete insight, design validation, and organizational decision-making. That is how brands get faster without surrendering technical credibility or brand focus.

For larger footwear organizations, this is a useful benchmark: separate where brand economics truly differ, and shorten the path from performance data to commercialized product. AI matters here only when it cuts cycle time or improves design confidence.

Source quality is strong overall but not perfectly even. Walmart and Target items are first-party disclosures and earnings materials, so they are strongest on what each company launched and how each described performance. The ASICS items are also first-party and specific, but some of the product-development claims are forward-looking and should be treated as capability signals rather than proven market outcomes.
