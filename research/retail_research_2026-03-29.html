Retail Intelligence Pulse

Sunday, March 29 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

Executive summary

No major new overnight disclosure from the core footwear set changed the board. The real new signal is structural: AI commerce is being forced into a more realistic shape.

OpenAI is explicitly shifting ChatGPT shopping toward product discovery and away from owning checkout, while Walmart is moving into an in-ChatGPT experience that keeps account linking, loyalty, and payments under Walmart control. Separately, a WTO subgroup moved ahead with the first baseline digital trade rules, which matters for the medium-term operating environment for cross-border digital commerce.

Bottom line: discovery may fragment across AI surfaces, but conversion, loyalty, payments, and service are re-consolidating around retailers and brands that control the transaction layer and data model.

What actually changed or newly matters now

OpenAI made the pivot explicit: ChatGPT shopping is now centered on richer product discovery, not OpenAI-owned checkout.
Walmart is one of the clearest retailer responses so far: it now has an in-ChatGPT experience that preserves retailer-owned identity, loyalty, and payment rails.
The WTO move is easy to ignore but strategically real: baseline digital trade rules are advancing even without full consensus, which matters for the future plumbing of global ecommerce and marketplace operations.

Source-backed facts

AI commerce: discovery is opening up, but checkout control is snapping back to retailers

Fact: OpenAI said this week it is making shopping in ChatGPT more visual and comparison-oriented, with users able to browse products, compare options side by side, and refine results conversationally.
Fact: OpenAI said it is expanding its Agentic Commerce Protocol to support product discovery and stated that merchants can share product feeds and promotions so their catalogs are represented inside ChatGPT.
Fact: OpenAI said it found the initial version of Instant Checkout did not provide enough flexibility, so merchants are being allowed to use their own checkout experiences while OpenAI focuses on discovery.
Fact: OpenAI said retailers including Target, Sephora, Nordstrom, Lowe’s, Best Buy, The Home Depot, and Wayfair have integrated into ACP for discovery.
Fact: OpenAI said Walmart is introducing an in-ChatGPT app experience that supports account linking, loyalty, and Walmart payments.
Fact: CNBC separately reported that OpenAI pivoted after Instant Checkout struggled with merchant onboarding, accurate product data, multi-item carts, and loyalty connections.

Digital trade rules: the background rails are shifting too

Fact: Reuters reported on March 28 that a group of WTO members agreed to move ahead with the world’s first baseline digital trade rules among consenting participants after broader WTO incorporation was blocked.
Fact: Reuters said the participating members represent about 70% of global trade and that 66 members settled on an interim arrangement to activate the deal within their own countries.
Fact: Reuters said the agreement is aimed at fostering a more open environment for digital trade, though the United States is not among the 66 signatories and its position remains under review.

Why this matters for footwear and adjacent retail now

Fact: Target’s March strategy update said it is investing incremental capital and operating spend to strengthen digital discovery, loyalty and engagement, and AI-enabled personalization—exactly the capabilities that become more important if discovery disperses beyond owned web and app traffic.

Implications for footwear digital

The market is moving toward a two-layer commerce model. AI assistants can win top-of-funnel discovery and comparison, but retailers and brands still need to own the systems that matter commercially: identity, membership, pricing logic, checkout, fulfillment promises, and post-purchase service.
This is bad news for weak product data and shallow CRM. If a brand’s catalog, attributes, availability, promotions, and membership logic are messy, it will lose visibility on AI surfaces and still fail to convert once the shopper lands on its controlled experience.
For footwear, the threat is not that ChatGPT becomes the store. The threat is that AI becomes the new merchandising shelf and comparison engine upstream of your store, reducing direct traffic advantage unless your assortment, feeds, and loyalty hooks are machine-readable and differentiated.
The WTO item is not an immediate sales catalyst, but it matters. Over time, clearer digital trade rules can shape how cross-border data, digital services, and marketplace operations scale. That is strategically relevant for global footwear brands even if the commercial impact is not immediate.

Actions

1. Audit AI-surface readiness, not just SEO readiness.
Pressure-test whether priority footwear products have clean attributes, current prices, review signals, inventory status, and promotional metadata that can travel accurately into AI discovery layers.
2. Treat loyalty and account linking as strategic infrastructure.
Walmart’s move is the tell: if discovery happens off-site, the winner is the retailer that can reattach the shopper to identity, benefits, and payment without adding friction.
3. Separate AI discovery experiments from checkout control.
Do not hand over the transaction layer casually. Test AI discovery aggressively, but keep commercial control over checkout, service, and customer data wherever possible.
