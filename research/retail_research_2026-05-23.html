Retail Intelligence Pulse

Saturday, May 23 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

The clearest fresh pattern from this week is that physical retail infrastructure is not being defended as legacy overhead. It is being funded as the engine of digital growth. Walmart's latest quarter showed local fulfillment speed, ecommerce growth, advertising, and membership scaling together. Target is committing more capital to stores, fulfillment, and delivery reach because it increasingly treats stores as the operating base for digital demand capture. At the same time, Deckers showed HOKA can still grow internationally, in DTC, and in wholesale without resorting to a weaker price posture. For footwear leaders, the message is blunt: digital advantage now depends as much on network design and channel discipline as on front-end experience.

Target is spending like stores are digital infrastructure

Capex, remodels, and faster delivery are being tied directly to digital convenience

Target said on May 11 it is investing about $5 billion in 2026 across more than 130 remodels, 30 new stores, and updates in technology and supply chain, explicitly arguing that stores power its same-day fulfillment services.

Target: store remodel and new-store strategy

Target also said in a fact sheet updated May 18 that next-day delivery of online orders is expanding so that 60 percent of the U.S. population will have access this spring.

Target: next-day delivery fact sheet

In its May 20 first-quarter earnings materials, Target said digital comparable sales grew 8.9 percent, with same-day delivery up more than 27 percent, while non-merchandise revenue streams including marketplace, membership, and retail media grew nearly 25 percent.

Target: Q1 2026 earnings release

Target: Q1 2026 earnings highlights

This is more important than another store refresh story. Target is treating physical assets, labor, and delivery radius as conversion infrastructure. That matters for footwear because the retailer that makes discovery, pickup, and replenishment feel easiest can win share before brand preference gets a vote.

Treat key retail partners' store networks as strategic media and fulfillment assets. Reassess where footwear launches, seasonal depth, and family-oriented assortments should concentrate if the real advantage is local convenience plus digital conversion, not just shelf space.

Walmart keeps widening the operating gap

Local fulfillment speed and higher-margin platform revenue are compounding together

Walmart reported on May 21 that global ecommerce sales grew 26 percent in Q1 FY27, global advertising grew 37 percent, and membership income rose 17.4 percent.

Walmart: Q1 FY27 earnings

Walmart said more than 36 percent of Walmart U.S. store-fulfilled delivery orders in the quarter were delivered in under three hours, and that about 50 percent of Walmart U.S. ecommerce fulfillment center volume is now automated.

Walmart: Q1 FY27 earnings

The company also said Sam's Club U.S. expedited delivery now reaches 65 percent of U.S. households, with millennial and Gen Z members accounting for roughly half of new members.

Walmart: Q1 FY27 earnings

Walmart is not just getting more efficient. It is building a stronger flywheel between speed, frequency, membership, and monetization. For brands, that raises the risk that wholesale partners become better than owned channels at capturing demand, closing the sale, and monetizing attention around the sale.

Segment wholesale partners by operating sophistication. If a partner can combine fast local fulfillment, retail media, and dense membership behavior, it deserves a more deliberate marketplace and assortment strategy than partners still selling mostly on traffic and promotions.

Deckers still shows premium footwear growth does not require channel compromise

HOKA is expanding globally while DTC and wholesale both remain productive

Deckers reported on May 21 that fourth-quarter net sales rose 9.6 percent to $1.119 billion, HOKA net sales increased 14.5 percent to $671.2 million, DTC net sales rose 13.2 percent, DTC comparable net sales increased 8.2 percent, wholesale net sales rose 7.1 percent, and international net sales grew 25.5 percent.

Deckers: Q4 and FY2026 results

For fiscal 2027, Deckers guided to total net sales of $5.86 billion to $5.91 billion and said HOKA is expected to grow by a low-double-digit percentage.

Deckers: Q4 and FY2026 results

That is still one of the strongest current footwear signals because it shows a premium brand can expand through both wholesale and owned channels without obviously sacrificing price architecture. In a market where many brands still need promotions to create motion, that is a meaningful competitive separation.

Use HOKA as a benchmark for channel health, not just product heat. Check whether your own DTC productivity, wholesale selectivity, and international sequencing are reinforcing each other or quietly fighting each other.

Evidence caveat: the core facts here come from first-hand company disclosures from Target, Walmart, and Deckers, which are strong for reported numbers and stated strategy but still self-interested. The interpretation that stores are now the operating center of digital growth is mine, inferred from how these companies are allocating capital and describing performance rather than from a single explicit industry statement.
