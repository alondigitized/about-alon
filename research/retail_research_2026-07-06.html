Retail Intelligence Pulse

Monday, July 06 2026 · Footwear, digital commerce, marketplaces

What newly matters now

The clearest retail signal entering the week is not another splashy AI demo. It is that three different operators are tightening the same commercial loop. Target is extending curated marketplace inventory into AI-led discovery while preserving loyalty identity. Walmart is organizing media, marketplace, membership and ecommerce around shared first-party measurement. On is investing in the wholesale operating layer itself: allocation, field enablement and B2B inventory visibility. For a footwear leader, that points to a harder truth: assortment curation, partner data quality and identity-linked conversion are no longer separate workstreams. They are the same profit system.

Target is turning curated marketplace growth into an identity-linked discovery engine

On July 1, Target said Target Plus is adding brands including Clarks, Forever 21, JanSport, JLab, Hisense and Serta, while keeping the marketplace invite-only.

Target Plus growth update, July 1, 2026

Target also said Target Plus items are showing up more clearly in AI-powered search and other digital discovery experiences through work with OpenAI, Google and Microsoft.

Target Plus growth update, July 1, 2026

Separately, on June 18, Target said more of the shopping journey can now happen inside conversational interfaces, including browsing, building multi-item baskets and linking to Target Circle loyalty accounts; in Google Search and Gemini, shoppers can browse Target listings, sign in to Target and complete purchases.

Target conversational AI fact sheet, June 18, 2026

This is not just marketplace expansion. Target is trying to make third-party assortment discoverable inside AI environments without surrendering loyalty identity or checkout control. For footwear brands, the bar rises on feed quality, variant hygiene, promo logic and inventory confidence because machine-led discovery will expose any weakness faster than classic site search did.

Pressure-test which footwear SKUs are truly ready for AI-led partner discovery. Clean up naming, size and width logic, availability feeds, image consistency and member-price eligibility before pushing harder into marketplace or conversational commerce surfaces.

Walmart is treating media, marketplace and membership as one measurement system

On June 22, Walmart said it is aligning Walmart Connect U.S., Walmart Connect International and Sam's Club through shared technology, tools, platforms and capabilities to speed innovation and create a more consistent advertiser experience.

Walmart commerce media update, June 22, 2026

Walmart said it connects the customer journey across stores, ecommerce, marketplaces, membership, connected TV and offsite media, and that the underlying advantage is first-party signals that improve activation, measurement and optimization across channels.

Walmart commerce media update, June 22, 2026

Walmart also said global advertising revenue grew 37% in its most recent quarter, while Walmart Connect U.S. grew 44% excluding VIZIO.

Walmart commerce media update, June 22, 2026

The strategic message is that Walmart no longer sees retail media as a side business. It is building a closed measurement loop across traffic, marketplace assortment, membership behavior and store demand. That matters in footwear because brands that still manage media, digital shelf, sell-through and wholesale support as separate teams will be slower than the platforms now selling and measuring them as one system.

Reframe retailer media discussions around operating visibility, not just ROAS. Ask what partner signals can be tied back to assortment productivity, new-buyer acquisition, marketplace conversion and in-store demand transfer by category and key franchise.

On is investing in wholesale execution instead of treating it like a passive channel

On said July 3 that its next phase of growth includes new investments designed to strengthen the global run specialty channel.

On strategy update, July 3, 2026

On said its summit brought together more than 200 influential run specialty partners, and that it is launching three support initiatives for those accounts: expanded launch exclusives and priority access, a broader Tech Rep footprint to educate store staff, and continued B2B investment to simplify pre-booking, improve inventory visibility and accelerate restocking.

On strategy update, July 3, 2026

On also said it opened a second LightSpray robot production facility near Busan, South Korea in April 2026 after opening its first facility in Zurich in 2025.

On strategy update, July 3, 2026

On is making an explicit bet that premium running growth will come from better partner economics and cleaner operating handoffs, not from flooding distribution. That should worry slower incumbents. In performance footwear, specialty wholesale is being upgraded from a sell-in outlet to a controlled demand-and-data surface.

Rank specialty and wholesale partners by strategic operating value, not just volume. The accounts worth disproportionate allocation are the ones that can absorb launch discipline, execute staff education, restock quickly and share enough signal to improve planning.

Source quality is strong on the hard facts because the brief relies on first-party disclosures from Target, Walmart and On published between June 18 and July 3, 2026. The interpretation is mine. The main caveat is recency: there was no major new overnight filing from Nike, adidas or other primary footwear names by early Monday, July 6, 2026, so this brief emphasizes the most strategically meaningful disclosures that now form a clearer pattern.
